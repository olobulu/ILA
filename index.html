Sustainable forest management & Protection

ILA offers services for the sustainable management and protection of Naimina'enkiyio Forest, blending traditional knowledge with modern techniques to preserve this natural ecosystem for future generations.

VOICE OF THE YOUTH IN ISUPUKI

ILA champions the voice of the youth in Isupukii, empowering young people to actively participate in governance and decision-making processes. We provide platforms and opportunities for the youth to influence policies and contribute to community development.

Community Forest Watchdog

ILA ensures that forest investors benefit the community and sustain the environment through vigilant oversight and advocacy. Our Community Forest Watchdog service monitors and evaluates forestry projects to ensure they align with environmental sustainability and community welfare goals.

Our Story

Founded in 2024, ILA is an association created by youth from the four Isupukii regions to promote unity and tackle shared challenges. By working together, ILA empowers young people to address community issues and strengthen their collective voice.


ILA is dedicated to safeguarding community natural forest, ensuring that development projects and employment opportunities are equitable and sustainable, and holding forest investors accountable to community and environmental standards. Through these efforts, ILA aims to drive positive change and foster a thriving, engaged community.

MAA TRADITIONAL MEDICINE SPECIALIST

A Maa Traditional Specialist is an expert in Maasai traditional medicine. They use herbal remedies for healing and provide spiritual guidance. Their deep knowledge of Maasai traditions and natural resources makes them essential to the health and well-being of their people.

LAIBON SPECIALIST

A Laibon is a respected Maasai spiritual leader and healer. They specialize in traditional medicine, using herbal remedies for healing, and provide spiritual guidance and counsel to the community. Their deep knowledge of Maasai culture and natural resources is vital to the well-being of their people.

MAASAI TRADITIONAL ENTERTAINMENT

Maasai traditional entertainment includes vibrant singing and dancing, such as the famous jumping dance "Adumu," and storytelling by elders. It also features intricate beadwork and crafting, as well as ceremonial celebrations with music, dance, and feasting.

ILA FOREST CONSERVATION

Location

The ILA Conservation is situated at the edges of the Naimina'enkiyio Forest, near the forest entrance in Morijo location.

Campsite

The ILA Conservation features over six campsites, providing ample opportunities for visitors to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the Naimina'enkiyio Forest.

Forest Investors

So far, we have three dedicated investors in the community forest who have been instrumental in providing employment opportunities to the villages and supporting various community projects.

what is there

Our conservation area is a haven of natural beauty and biodiversity. It is characterized by majestic tall trees that form a lush canopy, providing a habitat for a variety of wildlife. Among the many species that call this area home are elephants, whose presence adds to the richness of the ecosystem. Visitors can enjoy a cool and serene environment, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and those seeking tranquility amidst nature's splendor.

INTERIM TEAM

The Interim Committee of the Isupukii (ILA) was established from the four Isupukii regions to guide the Association through its initial stages. This committee is responsible for setting up foundational structures, creating strategies for unity and addressing common issues, and ensuring smooth operations until a permanent leadership team is elected. Their duties include organizing meetings, overseeing projects, and facilitating communication among members to achieve the association's goals. The Interim Committee will be dissolved once a stable, permanent governance structure is in place.
